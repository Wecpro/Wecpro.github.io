Lumio English Writing-Unit One Lecture

Lumio English Level II - English Writing Unit 1（BE verbs&Personal Pronoun）

Course Length：10/31/2020 - 12/19/2020

Unit Summary：This unit topic is self-introduction, the lectures mainly focus on the common grammar and sentence structures student use during self-introduction, which include:

  1. Understand first person sigular pronoun in both sujective case and objective case.(I,me)
  2. Introduce first person singular possessive adjectives and its grammar rules.(my)
  3. Understand and distinguish the ‘Be’ verbs “am”, “is”, and “are”.
  4. Understand the plural forms of ‘Be’ verbs.
  5. Understand the basic grammar rules for ‘Be’ verbs and related sentence structure.
  6. Understand the grammar rules for present progressive tense and related sentence structures.

Click to Download Powerpoint:

1. Lesson 1
 - Lesson Summary：Go over course syllabus, introduce the grammar rules for ‘Be’ verbs and related sentence structure, introduce first person singular pronoun “I” and its possessive adjective “my”.
2. Lesson 2
 - Lesson Summary：Review the grammar rules for ‘Be’ verbs, introduce the grammar rules for the plural froms of ‘Be’ verbs, distinguish the first person pronouns “I” and “my”.
3. Lesson 3
 - Lesson Summary：Review both the plural and singular forms of be verbs and the grammer rules for the first person pronouns, introduce the grammar rules for present progressive tense and related sentence structures.
4. Lesson 4
 - Lesson Summary：Review the grammer rules for present progressive tense and related sentence structures, introduce the objective case of the personal pronoun “I”.
5. Lesson 5
 - Lesson Summary：Review all grammars covered in Unit 1, including the basic grammar rules for ‘Be” verbs and their plural and singular forms, first personal pronouns, the objective case of the personal pronouns, and the present progressive tense.
6. Writing-Unit1Test
 - Lesson Summary：Unit 1 test, which covers the basic grammar rules for ‘Be” verbs and their plural and singular forms, first personal pronouns, the objective case of the personal pronouns, and the present progressive tense.
 - Answer Key： Writing-Unit1Test(key)
